What is Apologetics?


Apologetics is looking at the outside world and utilizing science and logic to defend one's faith. An important note is the definition of "faith." The most applicable definition, from Dictionary.com, is as follows: "belief that is not based on proof." Doesn't this contradict the very definition of Apologetics? By no means! Faith is required for any worldview. Theists have faith that there is a being beyond the material universe. Atheists have faith that it was nothing that caused everything. Defining one's faith is not definitively proving anything. Rather, it is being able to articulate why it is you believe your faith is more reasonable than another's.

Why is Apologetics Important?

There is a common misconception which has been perpetuated through popular media as well as academic institutions: Science has buried G-d. In other words science has, seemingly, nullified the necessity of there being a god-like being. Evidence being put forth as nails in G-d's proverbial coffin have been things ranging from evolution to the idea of aliens spreading life to other planets.


Who is Apologetics For?

Apologetics is for everyone! It is for people wishing to learn to defend their faith. It is for people curious about Christianity or other faiths. It is even for people who wish to argue against Christianity to become more familiar with the arguments of their counterparts.


Our Mission?

To spur people towards digging into science and logic to make their decisions rather than making decisions and then looking at the evidence to back yourself up.
To bring knowledge and science to people in a simple and engaging way.
To point people towards the existence of G-d through means most think disprove Him.


What Evidence is there that G-d Exists?

If the universe had a beginning, who started it? Where did the design of the universe come from? Where did life come from? Where does our conscience and moral compass come from?


What Evidence is there Backing the New Testament?

Accuracy of the Scriptures and their translation.
The honesty of the New Testament authors.
Evidence that Jesus did die and rose from the grave.
