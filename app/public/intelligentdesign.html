Intelligent Design

1. Everything that has a design has a designer.
2. Life has a design.
3. Life has a designer.

Life is spectacular to spectate, from the intricacies of social interaction down to the interactions between molecules in a cell.
It is difficult to look at the reality around us and not see our designed nature.
To quote Douglas Axe, we have a "design intuition."

Take, for example, proteins. Altering one or two amino acids within a protein can totally destroy its functionality, thus rendering it useless.
Not only is it now useless, but it could be important enough that the organism cannot survive without it.
The likelihood of proteins evolving into what they are now is more unlikely than finding the center of the CUDA intersection while blindly traveling the world in search for it.
