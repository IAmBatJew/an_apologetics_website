N: No Evidence of Falsehood. No Evidence of Falsehood. There is no known incentive for the apostles to lie about the resurrection and every incentive to not proclaim the resurrection. . One of the chief reasons to believe that the apostles honestly believed Jesus rose from the dead is letter E in the acronym, but another reason is that the story of a crucified, risen Messiah goes against Jewish expectations of the Messiah and a story of a crucified, risen deity would go against Roman expectations. If the Apostles were dishonest and trying to craft a religion, we would expect them to craft a story that falls more in line with cultural norms and expectations.

I: Interactions Post-Ressurection. There are numerous reports of people seeing Jesus after he died. Many of these accounts happen in group settings where multiple people are experiencing the same thing. This is important because it rules out the hallucination theory that posits the idea that the disciples were just hallucinating.

PLACEHOLDER

C: Conversions. Another reason to believe that Jesus rose from the dead, is that key people who were hostile to Christianity such as the Apostle Paul and James, the brother of Jesus, converted to Christianity. The fact that their were contemporary people who were hostile to Christianity and converted after having a post resurrection appearance of Jesus, strengthens the idea that Jesus actually rose again.

PLACEHOLDER

E: Ensured Death. Jesus suffered one of the worst forms of execution at the time. His disciples were at risk of suffering this same fate if they proclaimed the resurrection. Many of the disciples did end up suffering the the death penalty for proclaiming the resurrection and yet none of the apostles recanted or went back on their claim. This shows that the disciples really believed they saw Jesus after his death. How did the disciples come to believe Jesus rose from the dead? The theory that best fits all the facts is that the disciples came to believe in the resurrection because Jesus actually rose from the dead.
